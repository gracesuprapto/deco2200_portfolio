Week 1

During this week, we formed groups in our tutorials and discussed problem areas and our interests in a specific autonomous product, specifically vehicles. When forming groups, it was important to choose people that would each individually contribute their own skills and knowledge to ensure that we worked well as a team to come up with a solution. I chose people to work with that were better in a specific skill and area that I personally lacked in. Elaine’s strength is her ability to use Sketch and create wireframes. Miriam is able to create prototype and her skills in using the various tools will be a big help for our project. We named our group GEM, for Grace; Elaine and Miriam.

Figure 1.1 - GEM doing background research

We proceeded to do initial research and from it found that we were fascinated with autonomous buses as it is an area that is used very often and commonly by most people. Therefore, it would be an area that could be worked upon and researched further as it allows for more people to identify with it and relate to. It is also a problem area that could be solved through many different avenues and possibilities. We looked into many different areas such as education, health and education. Tossing around ideas such as creating a system that would allow efficient mobilisation of school children, tourism vehicles that allowed for easier transportation of tourists to move from one attraction to another and the movement of patients within hospitals. We looked into problem areas within these sectors and the more research we did to discover problems, allowed us to narrow down and weigh the pros and cons of the different sectors.

Week 2

After our initial primitive research and weighing the various pros and cons of the different sectors, we decided to continue with the tourism sector, specifically looking into group charters and the movement of tourists. By choosing the tourism industry, it is easily identifiable as travel and tourism is a common part of people’s lives as many have travelled before and travelling is on the rise. We looked into existing tourism companies such as the Hop on Hop off Bus. They are a commonly used and chosen way of transportation around famous landmarks in major cities such as New York, London and function in cities around Australia including Sydney.

After defining the problem space, the boundary in which we would work in, tourism, and did our background research, we were able to understand a basic need of what people needed. We also wrote out our stakeholder map to see what the direct and indirect people this sector and section would affect. From both research and pre-existing knowledge, we create a list of core, direct and indirect stakeholders. We brainstormed a list of stakeholders and

then categorised them into where they belonged by level of influence, starting with those directed affected and worked our way out to indirect stakeholders. We wanted to ensure we identified a target audience and specific group of people that we believed would be interested in our final autonomous product/service.

Week 3

Figure 3.1 - Divided list of research method between the group, categorised by colour

This week, we discussed within our group the different user research methods we could use. The methods we came up with were, observations; interviews; questionnaires; context mapping; background research; metaphor design; experience prototyping; forced associations; Mindmapping; roleplay; scenarios; focus groups and contextual observations. From this list we divided it up so that we did not all do the same research methods. By varying the methods we could gain a deeper and richer understanding of our user through various promptings and ways in which they would be understood.

Previous Next

The research method I chose was questionnaire, roleplay, focus groups and contextual observations. I chose to do questionnaires allows for responses that hone in on users’ initial and basic reactions to questions that prompt their thoughts on past and personal experiences through clear research questions. Questions are easy to answer and record which allows me to get larger amount of responses from a wider range of people. I also chose to do roleplay as it would show me how people react in a situation that is monitored and created. As autonomous vehicles are not used widely, by creating a make belief environment it would allow for a better perspective and to observe how people react to the situation. Focus groups allows for me to gain responses in a controlled environment where I can hear people’s thoughts and further ask questions about a specific topic that is prompted. Contextual observation will give me first hand understandings and findings of the physical behaviour a user may have while doing a task at a certain location. The location that I’ll chose to observe will provide insight into how the user interacts with the surroundings as well as the service. I then created the survey. I made sure that I chose questions that wouldn’t take very long to answer to gain a general census and understanding of people’s general ideas of automated buses.I included questions such as what the types of tours interested them the most and who they would travel with when visiting in a new place.

After re-evaluating the user research methods I was planning to use, I decided to choose to do different methods as I felt as though they would suit the data I was looking for more. So the final research methods I chose to use were questionnaires, interviews and contextual observation. Interviews allow for a more in depth question and answer. It provides a qualitative understanding of the user’s wants, needs, frustrations and certain behaviours, compared to surveys which provide quantitative data.From the data collected from interviews, I used affinity diagrams for context inquiry to analyse and further understand the specific needs and wants of the user to create two personas.
